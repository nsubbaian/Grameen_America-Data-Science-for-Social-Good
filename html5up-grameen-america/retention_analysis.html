The Goal

Grameen America works to provide loans for women entrepreneurs who want to grow their business. The participants in the program can choose to stay as long as they want, incrementally asking for higher loans as they pay them back. Grameen America would like to determine the variables that help predict which participants stay with them longer.


What variables help explain retention?

All participants in Grameen America were asked to take a Questionnaire before and after receiving their loans. All this data was integrated into a dataset which was analyzed to understand if there were certain variables that affected retention more than others.

Shown bellow is the information included in the dataset as well as how retention was defined.

Metrics of Retention

In order to predict retention from the variables, retention must be defined. Traditionally, retention means those who stick with the program; however in this case, it may be considered a good thing if the participant decides to leave the program (they succesfully were able to grow their business and no longer needed support). So we decided to define retention as those who stick with the program. As "retention" does not have a concrete definition, we experimented with three ways to define retention, to figure out which definition best described those whose stayed with the Grameen program. Shown bellow are the three retention metrics.

Metric 1: Person is considered retained if they have a previously closed loan account OR closed loan

Metric 2: Person is considered retained if they have more than 2 completed cycles

 Metric 3: There are 3 categories of retention/experience that a person can fall under.
1. No Retention --> 0-2 completed loan cycles (~41% of members)
2. Retention --> 3-9 completed loan cycles (~43% of members)
 3. Long Term Retention --> 10 or more completed loan cycles (~15% of members)

From our analysis of the above retention metrics, we chose metric 3. In general, Metric 1 produced differing results or no trends, while Metric 2 and 3 produced similar results. As Retention 3 showed levels of retention, we decided to use metric 3 for the rest of our analysis.
